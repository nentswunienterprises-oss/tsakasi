Tsa Kasi Logistics Business Model
Energy-Optimized Regional Logistics Infrastructure for Local Commerce
Operating Company within the Nenterprises (Pty) Ltd Ecosystem

1. Business Model Summary
Tsa Kasi Logistics (Pty) Ltd is a regional logistics infrastructure company built to help businesses and platforms move goods more cost-effectively, reliably, and visibly at the local last-mile level.
Tsa Kasi does not exist because logistics companies do not exist. Formal logistics companies already operate in the market. The problem is more specific and more economically important:
The existing movement layer is expensive because most local and last-mile logistics still depend heavily on fuel-based movement economics.
For restaurants, pharmacies, retailers, local SMEs, regional commerce operators, national courier networks, and e-commerce platforms, the issue is not simply whether delivery is available. The issue is whether local last-mile delivery can be made affordable, reliable, trackable, and economically sustainable when goods reach the customer-door level.
Tsa Kasi Logistics enters the market to build a lower-cost, energy-optimized B2B movement layer for regional commerce.
The company combines:
electric fleet infrastructure,
merchant delivery execution,
platform last-mile fulfillment,
route density,
dispatch discipline,
OTP-verified delivery,
proof-of-delivery systems,
fleet utilization tracking,
energy cost visibility,
and operating software through Relief Works Technologies.
Tsa Kasi is not positioned as a food delivery app, consumer marketplace, or informal courier operation.
It is positioned as:
Regional Clean Logistics Infrastructure for Local Commerce.
The company’s real product is not “deliveries.”
The real product is:
Low-cost, reliable commerce movement.
2. Core Business Thesis
The central thesis of Tsa Kasi Logistics is:
Regional commerce does not only need access to logistics. It needs a more cost-efficient movement layer.
Local movement is one of the most expensive and difficult parts of commerce because the final delivery leg is operationally fragmented, fuel-sensitive, and highly local.
Fuel-based movement makes this worse.
Every delivery carries an energy cost. Every route, delay, idle trip, return trip, failed delivery, and inefficient dispatch decision increases that cost.
As fuel prices fluctuate, delivery economics become harder to control. Merchants, courier operators, and platforms either absorb the cost, pass it on to customers, reduce margins, or limit delivery coverage.
Tsa Kasi Logistics is built to solve this by creating a cleaner, more systemized, energy-aware logistics execution layer.
The long-term business opportunity is not merely to complete deliveries.
The opportunity is to reduce the cost and friction of local commerce movement.

10. Pricing Logic
Tsa Kasi’s pricing must balance affordability for customers with enough margin to sustain the fleet, drivers, dispatch, software, and operations.
Pricing should consider:
distance,
delivery urgency,
merchant or platform volume,
payload,
vehicle type required,
delivery window,
return trip risk,
failed delivery risk,
SLA requirements,
proof-of-delivery requirements,
route density,
and monthly commitment.
The company may use different pricing models.
10.1 Flat Local Delivery Fee
A fixed fee within a defined local radius.
Best for simplicity and early merchant adoption.
10.2 Distance-Based Fee
Pricing increases as delivery distance increases.
Best for fairness and cost control.
10.3 Subscription + Discounted Delivery Fee
Merchant pays monthly, then gets lower per-delivery rates.
Best for recurring merchant relationships.
10.4 Route Contract Pricing
Customer pays for a fixed recurring route or delivery window.
Best for predictable movement.
10.5 Platform Per-Parcel Pricing
A platform or courier network pays per successfully completed local delivery.
Best for parcel networks and e-commerce platforms.
10.6 Platform Node-Capacity Pricing
A larger platform pays for access to delivery capacity in a specific node.
Best for customers that need reliable regional coverage without directly operating the last-mile fleet.
10.7 Enterprise or B2B Custom Pricing
For larger partners with higher complexity.
Best for platforms, pharmacies, retailers, courier networks, and institutional contracts.

The economic engine of Tsa Kasi is based on improving delivery economics through density, fleet utilization, and energy cost reduction.
The core formula is:
Customer density increases route density. Route density increases fleet utilization. Fleet utilization lowers cost per delivery. Electric fleet infrastructure reduces fuel exposure. Software improves dispatch efficiency. Together, the movement layer becomes more cost-efficient.
Tsa Kasi should track these metrics from the beginning:
revenue per delivery,
cost per delivery,
energy cost per delivery,
average delivery distance,
average delivery time,
driver cost per delivery,
vehicle utilization per day,
deliveries per vehicle per day,
deliveries per merchant per month,
deliveries per platform per month,
failed delivery rate,
repeat merchant usage,
platform delivery volume,
merchant retention,
gross margin per delivery,
contribution margin per customer,
and route density by area.
The key investor metric is not only total delivery volume.
The key question is:
Does cost per delivery improve as the node becomes denser?
If yes, Tsa Kasi becomes an infrastructure business rather than a low-margin delivery operation.
